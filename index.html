THE JOURNEY

Your 15-Day Adventure - Day by Day

From the moment you land in Kathmandu to the moment you stand at 5,364m Everest Base Camp, every detail is handled.

01

Arrival in Kathmandu

Kathmandu - 1,400m - Hotel - Dinner

Arrive at Tribhuvan International Airport, Kathmandu. You will be received by our representative and transferred to your hotel. Evening welcome briefing, gear check, and team dinner.

02

Kathmandu Sightseeing & Trek Prep

Kathmandu - 1,400m - Hotel - Breakfast

Explore Kathmandu's UNESCO World Heritage Sites: Pashupatinath Temple, Boudhanath Stupa, and Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple). Final gear check and pre-trek briefing with your Sherpa guide.

03

Fly to Lukla - Trek to Phakding

Phakding - 2,610m - 3-4 hrs - Teahouse

Early morning scenic flight to Tenzing-Hillary Airport, Lukla (one of the world's most thrilling airports). Begin trekking through rhododendron forests along the Dudh Koshi River to Phakding.

04

Phakding to Namche Bazaar

Namche - 3,440m - 5-6 hrs - Teahouse

One of the most scenic days. Cross multiple suspension bridges over the Dudh Koshi River and enter Sagarmatha National Park. The final climb to Namche Bazaar is steep but the views of Everest and Lhotse make every step worthwhile.

05
Acclimatization

Acclimatization - Namche

Namche - 3,440m - Rest day - Teahouse

CRITICAL: This acclimatization day is non-negotiable for safety. Rest in Namche, explore the Saturday market, visit the Everest View Hotel for your first glimpse of Everest, Ama Dablam, and Lhotse.

06

Namche to Tengboche

Tengboche - 3,870m - 5-6 hrs - Teahouse

Trek through stunning Himalayan landscapes past Kyangjuma to Phunki Thanka. A steep ascent brings you to Tengboche, home to the famous Tengboche Monastery — the spiritual heart of the Khumbu region.

07

Tengboche to Dingboche

Dingboche - 4,410m - 5-6 hrs - Teahouse

Trek through the beautiful Imja Khola valley past Pangboche village. Views of Lhotse (world's 4th highest peak), Island Peak, and Makalu begin to emerge.

08
Acclimatization

Acclimatization - Dingboche

Dingboche - 4,410m - Rest day - Teahouse

Second critical acclimatization day. Optional hike to Nagarjun Hill (5,100m) for breathtaking 360° views of Makalu, Lhotse, Island Peak, and Ama Dablam.

09

Dingboche to Lobuche

Lobuche - 4,940m - 4-5 hrs - Teahouse

Trek past the Thukla Pass memorial — a moving tribute to climbers lost on Everest. The memorial stones mark one of the trek's most emotional and reflective moments.

10
The Big Day

Lobuche to EBC to Gorak Shep

EBC - 5,364m - 7-8 hrs - Teahouse

⭐ THE MOMENT EVERYTHING HAS BEEN BUILDING TOWARD ⭐ Trek from Lobuche to Gorak Shep, drop bags, and continue to EVEREST BASE CAMP at 5,364m. You will stand at the foot of the world's tallest mountain, surrounded by the Khumbu Glacier.

11

Kala Patthar Sunrise → Pheriche

KP - 5,545m - 7-8 hrs - Teahouse

Pre-dawn hike (3:30 AM start) to Kala Patthar (5,545m) for the most iconic Everest sunrise view in the world. Descend to Gorak Shep, then trek all the way down to Pheriche.

12

Pheriche to Namche

Namche - 3,440m - 6-7 hrs - Teahouse

Long descent day back toward Namche Bazaar. Your knees will feel the miles but the altitude drop brings energy and appetite back. Celebratory dinner in Namche.

13

Namche to Lukla

Lukla - 2,840m - 6-7 hrs - Teahouse

Final full trekking day. Descend from Namche through Monjo, Jorsale, and Phakding back to Lukla. Farewell dinner with your guide and porter team.

14

Fly Lukla to Kathmandu

Kathmandu - 1,400m - Hotel - Breakfast

Morning flight from Lukla back to Kathmandu. Transfer to hotel. Free afternoon for shopping, Thamel exploration, or simply celebrating with a hot shower and proper bed.

15

Departure Day

Kathmandu Airport - Breakfast

Final breakfast at the hotel. Our representative will transfer you to Tribhuvan International Airport. You leave Nepal transformed — carrying memories, photographs, and a story you'll tell for the rest of your life.

IS THE EBC TREK RIGHT FOR YOU?

How fit do I need to be?+

Walk 5-7 hrs/day, handle stairs comfortably, moderate hikes = candidate. 3-month training plan recommended.

I've never trekked before. Can I do this?+

Many first-timers complete EBC. Key is preparation. Full fitness guide sent upon booking.

What's the biggest challenge — fitness or altitude?+

Altitude is primary. Our 15-day itinerary maximises acclimatisation time. Fitness helps but is secondary.

Is there an age limit?+

Guided trekkers age 16 to 72. No official limit. Health and fitness matter more than age. Consult doctor if pre-existing conditions.

Every Question You're Thinking. Answered.

What exactly is included in the $1,430 price?+
Everything Kathmandu to Kathmandu — flights, permits, teahouse accommodation, 3 meals/day, guide, porter. See full includes list above.
How do I book?+
Inquiry via form or WhatsApp → confirm availability → complete booking form → $200 deposit secures your place → full payment due 45 days before departure.
What payment methods do you accept?+
Credit card (Visa/Mastercard), PayPal, and bank transfer (SEPA/SWIFT for EU).
Can I book a private departure?+
Yes. Private groups of 2-12 people on almost any date. Contact us for custom pricing.
Is travel insurance mandatory?+
YES. Your policy must cover helicopter evacuation (minimum $100,000) and high-altitude trekking. We can recommend providers.
How difficult is the EBC trek?+
Moderate to strenuous. Altitude is the bigger challenge — the trail itself is well-worn and non-technical. Daily walking 5-8 hours.
What is the maximum altitude?+
5,364m at Everest Base Camp. Kala Patthar (optional summit on Day 11) reaches 5,545m.
Will I definitely reach Base Camp?+
The majority of trekkers do. A small percentage are affected by altitude sickness — that's why our 2 acclimatisation days and daily oximeter monitoring are critical.
What are teahouses like?+
Basic but comfortable. Shared or private rooms (upgrades available). Hot meals served. Limited electricity and warm water above 4,000m. Wi-Fi available for a small fee.
What is the best season to trek?+
Spring (Mar-May): Best Everest summit views. Autumn (Sep-Nov): Clear skies, less crowded, fall colours. Both are excellent.
What gear do I need?+
Detailed packing list sent on booking. Essentials: trekking poles, down jacket (above 4,000m), warm sleeping bag, sturdy boots.
How should I train?+
Start 3 months ahead: weekly long hikes (3-5 hrs), stair climbing, cardio. Complete training guide sent upon booking.
Should I take altitude medication?+
Consult your doctor. Acetazolamide (Diamox) is commonly prescribed. Your guide monitors your oxygen daily with a pulse oximeter.
What vaccinations do I need?+
Consult a travel health clinic 6-8 weeks before: Hepatitis A, Typhoid, routine vaccinations. Malaria risk is low at high altitude.
Do I need a visa for Nepal?+
Most nationalities obtain a visa on arrival at Kathmandu Airport (~$30 for 15 days, $50 for 30 days). Check requirements for your nationality.
What currency should I bring?+
USD is widely accepted. Budget $200-$400 USD in small bills for personal expenses (hot drinks, charging, tips, extras).
How do I get to Kathmandu?+
Fly to Tribhuvan International Airport (KTM). Detailed travel advice sent upon booking.
Is there Wi-Fi and phone signal on the trail?+
Wi-Fi at most teahouses for a small fee (better at lower altitudes). We recommend an NTC or Ncell SIM with data — available at KTM airport.
How are porters treated and paid?+
Above TAAN recommended minimum wage. Maximum 25kg carry. Proper gear, clothing, and insurance provided. Non-negotiable standards.
What is your environmental policy?+
Leave No Trace on all treks. We carry out non-biodegradable waste, minimise single-use plastics, and recommend teahouses with responsible waste management.
Does my booking benefit local communities?+
Directly, yes. 100% locally owned. Every dollar stays in Nepal — employing local guides, supporting teahouse owners, contributing to community schools.
