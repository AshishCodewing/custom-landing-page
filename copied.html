THE JOURNEY

Your 15-Day Adventure — Day by Day

From the moment you land in Kathmandu to the moment you stand at 5,364m Everest Base Camp, every detail is handled.

Arrive at Tribhuvan International Airport, Kathmandu. You will be received by our representative and transferred to your hotel. Evening welcome briefing, gear check, and team dinner.

Explore Kathmandu's UNESCO World Heritage Sites: Pashupatinath Temple, Boudhanath Stupa, and Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple). Final gear check and pre-trek briefing with your Sherpa guide.

Early morning scenic flight to Tenzing-Hillary Airport, Lukla (one of the world's most thrilling airports). Begin trekking through rhododendron forests along the Dudh Koshi River to Phakding.

⭐ THE MOMENT EVERYTHING HAS BEEN BUILDING TOWARD ⭐ Trek from Lobuche to Gorak Shep, drop bags, and continue to EVEREST BASE CAMP at 5,364m. You will stand at the foot of the world's tallest mountain, surrounded by the Khumbu Glacier.

YOUR SAFETY IS OUR PRIORITY

Worried About Altitude? Here's Exactly How We Keep You Safe.

📊

Daily Oxygen Monitoring

Every trekker's blood oxygen level (SpO2) is checked daily with a pulse oximeter. If your reading drops below safe levels, we respond immediately — descent before symptoms worsen.

🔄

Two Acclimatization Days Built In

Unlike 14-day itineraries that rush acclimatization, our 15-day route includes deliberate rest days at Namche (3,440m) and Dingboche (4,410m) following the critical 'climb high, sleep low' protocol.

🚁

Emergency Evacuation Planning

Every trekker's insurance must cover helicopter evacuation before departure. We pre-identify the nearest landing zones at each overnight stop and maintain emergency contacts throughout the route.

💊

Guide First Aid Training

All guides complete NTB-certified wilderness first aid training and carry a full professional first aid kit. We are equipped to handle altitude sickness, injuries, and medical emergencies on the trail.

📈 Altitude Profile — Day by Day

IS THE EBC TREK RIGHT FOR YOU?

You don't need to be an elite athlete. If you can walk 5-7 hours a day, handle stairs comfortably, and have done moderate hikes, you are a candidate. We recommend a 3-month training plan before departure.

Many first-time trekkers complete EBC every year. The key is preparation. We provide a complete pre-trek fitness guide upon booking.

Altitude affects everyone differently and is the primary reason some trekkers don't reach Base Camp. Our 15-day itinerary gives your body maximum time to acclimatize. Fitness helps but is secondary to pacing.

We've guided trekkers from age 16 to 72. There's no official age limit. Your health and fitness level matter more than your age. Consult your doctor before booking if you have any pre-existing conditions.
